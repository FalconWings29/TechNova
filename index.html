Our Impact

Why TechNova Matters

Alameda County is full of curious, capable young people. The challenge is not a lack of resources but a lack of spark. Too many students have never had a reason to explore what technology can do or what they can build with it. TechNova exists to change that, by making hands-on technical learning so engaging and welcoming that students can't help but want to dive deeper. Every program is free. Every student is welcome.

About TechNova

TechNova was founded in 2025 by five high school students at The Quarry Lane School who wanted to use their technical skills to make a real difference in their community, and who believed that the best way to do that was to ignite curiosity in the next generation of builders and makers.

Our Story

Five Students, One Mission

TechNova began in 2025 as an idea shared by five high schoolers at The Quarry Lane School in Dublin, California. We had all fallen in love with programming, CAD, and making things, and we kept asking ourselves the same question: why aren't more kids around us interested in this?

We realized the issue was not access. It was exposure and excitement. So we decided to do something about it. We founded TechNova to bring the hands-on, creative side of technology directly to students across Alameda County, and to show them how genuinely fun and empowering it can be to build something from scratch.

Mission Statement

A Promise to Alameda County's
Next Generation

"TechNova exists to foster curiosity in every young person in Alameda County, equipping them with the technical skills, creative confidence, and hands-on experience to discover just how exciting it is to build, design, and create, regardless of their background or prior experience."
01

Access

We remove every barrier between students and quality technical education. All programs are free of charge. We provide equipment, materials, and transportation support where needed.

02

Excellence

Our curricula are developed by industry professionals and aligned with real-world skills. We hold ourselves to the same standards as the best STEM programs in the country.

03

Community

Strategic Goals

What We're Working Toward

01

Reach 2,000 Students by 2026

Expand our program footprint to every school district in Alameda County through new partnerships and satellite locations.

02

Launch 3 Community Labs

Open permanent, drop-in TechNova labs in Oakland, Fremont, and Hayward, equipped with 3D printers, computers, and fabrication tools.

03

Train 50 New Instructors

Build a robust volunteer instructor pipeline with structured onboarding and certification so our quality scales with our growth.

04

Develop Open Curriculum

Publish all TechNova curricula as open-source resources so other nonprofits and schools nationwide can replicate our model.
